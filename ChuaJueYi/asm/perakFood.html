Liew Kee Chee Cheong Fun


Chee Cheong Fun are the representative of Anshun cuisine, and the most famous one should be Liaoji Rice noodle rolls. Liaoji is open from the afternoon until it is sold out late at night, and there are still crowds of people even at night. Rice noodle rolls have two flavors: regular and dried shrimp. Many customers who come to try new things will order both flavors to try. The regular flavor is darker than the common rice noodle rolls. It contains dried shrimp, water chestnut, and onion, but there is no sauce. It is very delicious with pickled green peppers. The dried shrimp flavored rice noodle rolls are full of fried crispy dried shrimps. Those who like dried shrimps should not miss it.

Address: Jalan Hill, Pekan Teluk Intan, 36000 Teluk Intan, Perak, Malaysia

Kedai Biscuit Sin Guan Hoe


Sin Guan Hoe is a well-known bakery in the area. It insists on making and selling freshly made biscuits, and does not open branches for export, in order to better control the quality. The crust of the biscuits is crispy, and the maltose filling inside is slightly sweet with a sesame aroma. It is a specialty that many people buy when they go to Anshun.

Address: 23, Jalan Pasir Bedamar, Pekan Teluk Intan, 36000 Teluk Intan, Perak

Yau Kee Restaurant


The bread preserves the aroma of the curry chicken perfectly, and the aroma is really fragrant the moment you open it. The curry soup is also very good with rice, so many customers come to take away. In addition to breaded chicken, there are also different varieties such as curry breaded lamb and herbal breaded chicken for customers to choose from.

Address: 55 & 57, Jalan Idris, Kampung Masjid, 31900 Kampar, Perak, Malaysia

Restoran Chong Kee “Pokok Besar”


The biggest selling point is that the stuffed ingredients are all made and fried on the same day, and they taste very fresh. In addition, the roasted pork and red bean ice here are also recommended, which are good ingredients that attract people to come here.


Address: 1213, Jalan King, Taman Hoover, 31650 Ipoh, Perak, Malaysia

Fong Kee Mochi Peanut Paste


The mochi here is elastic and has a good texture. It is very happy to eat it with peanut crumbs and fine sugar (laugh). In addition, diners also recommend eating mochi with peanut paste, remember to try it!


Address: 550A, Jalan Queen, Pasir Pinji, 31050 Ipoh, Perak, Malaysia

Wanyuanfeng Bakery


In addition, the coconut dregs are stir-fried by hand, and the kaya is also boiled by hand, which tastes like old-fashioned bread. There are three types of bread sold here: coconut, kaya and no fillings. Because they are all handmade, only about 800 to 1,200 loaves are baked every day, and they are often sold out as soon as they come out of the oven.

Address: 50, Jalan Sultan, 31550 Pasir Pinji, Ipoh, Perak.

Taiping Famous PSL Goreng Pisang


The reason why PSL fried bananas are so popular among Taiping people is that the fried banana powder developed by the boss lady makes the bananas crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. The boss lady recommends that the most delicious ones are red bananas, but it is not easy to get them because the boss lady will not use those that are not ripe enough or too ripe, and even if there are any, they are likely to be ordered by regular customers. The price of red bananas is also more expensive than ordinary fried bananas, but it is still worth trying your luck for diners who come to Taiping. If you don't have them, you can also try the fried rice cakes, fried sweet potatoes, etc.

Address: 85, Jalan Pasar, 34000 Taiping, Perak, Malaysia

Setiawan Cheong Cia Gong Pian


The outside is sweet, but theirs is salty because alkaline water is added during the production process to prevent the cake from being fragile. It is also harder than the Guang Bing in Sibu, East Malaysia. Onions and lard are added to the Guang Bing, which tastes very fragrant. It is recommended to buy it while it is hot and eat it immediately. If you take it home, you can sprinkle some water on it and microwave it before eating. Since the number of baked goods is limited every day, friends who want to buy it remember to queue up early!

Address: 12, Lorong Tok Perdana, Taman Tok Perdana, 32000 Sitiawan, Perak, Malaysia
