Jonker-Street


Known for its lively night market, the street is lined with heritage buildings, antique shops, cafes, and street vendors, offering a wide range of local food, souvenirs, and handicrafts. By day, it's a historical hub, showcasing Malacca's rich cultural mix, while by night, it transforms into a bustling market with lights, music, and diverse culinary delights. Jonker Street is a must-visit for those looking to experience the charm and heritage of Malacca.

Afamosa


Built by the Portuguese in 1511 after they conquered Malacca, this fortress once stood as a symbol of their military strength. Today, only the small gatehouse known as Porta de Santiago remains, offering visitors a glimpse into the city’s colonial past. A Famosa is a key attraction, reflecting the blending of local and colonial histories, and it continues to draw visitors intrigued by Malacca’s rich and diverse heritage.

Shoer Sky Tower


Situated atop The Shore Shopping Gallery, this 42-floor tower gives visitors a bird's-eye view of iconic landmarks such as the Malacca River and St. Paul's Hill. The highlight of the experience is the glass-floor Sky Deck, where visitors can walk and feel like they're floating above the city offering a mix of modern attractions and scenic vistas in Malacca.

Melaka River


Once a bustling trade route during Malacca’s colonial era, the river has since been transformed into a charming attraction for visitors. The Melaka River Cruise offers a leisurely boat ride along the riverbanks, showcasing beautifully restored shophouses, murals, and landmarks like Kampung Morten, a traditional Malay village. Lined with vibrant cafes, street art, and colorful lights at night, the Melaka River is a peaceful and scenic spot that captures the essence of this UNESCO World Heritage city.

Christ Church


Built by the Dutch in 1753 to commemorate their century of rule in Malacca, this striking red-brick church is the oldest functioning Protestant church in Malaysia. Its distinctive features include a large white cross on the facade, Dutch-era tombstones, and an original handmade pew. Inside, the wooden beams were constructed without any nails, showcasing impressive craftsmanship. Christ Church stands as a testament to Malacca’s colonial past and is a must-visit for history enthusiasts and architecture lovers.
