Siam Road Char Kway Teow

The char kuey teow itself is full of wok hei and blessed with a distinctively fragrant taste thanks to the use of a duck egg. Of course, the additional ingredients of succulent mantis and regular prawns helps enhance the richness of the char kuey teow flavour.

Air Itam Bisu Laksa

A tangy hot soup with fat white noodles and sprinkles of tuna, this has a distinctive fragrance and is part of the authentic Penang delicacies. The sweet-sour taste will get you, or you may regret it all together, but it is worth a try nonetheless.
Authentic Qiaotou Fujian Noodles

Hokkien Mee, also known as Prawn Mee, is a popular Penang noodle dish that features a flavorful and aromatic broth made from prawn shells and heads, combined with thick yellow noodles and rice vermicelli. The dish is garnished with prawns, hard-boiled eggs, bean sprouts, water spinach, and a spoonful of spicy chili paste.
Penang Road’s famous Teochew Cendol

Natrah Nasi Kandar

Nasi Kandar consists of steamed rice combined with an array of distinct curries, sides dishes, and gravies. The selection of curries consists of various blends of vegetables, seafood or meat.
Famous Gani Pasembur

Pasembur is an Indian Muslim rojak consisting of shredded vegetables, eggs, tofu, and fritters drenched in a spiced sweet potato dressing.

Wantan Mee is a delicious serving of noodles in either dry or soup form. The dry variant uses a dark soy base while the soup traditionally comes with chicken or pork broth.

Bayan Baru Market Food Court

Fried Oyster Omelette, also known as ‘Oh Chien’ by local is one of the must-try street food in Penang. Comes with juicy, fresh oysters atop a bed of crispy yet sticky egg batter fried to perfection, this delicacy is guaranteed to please your taste buds, especially paired with the sour yet spicy chilli sauce.

Siam Road Char Kway Teow


This charcoal grilled fried kway teow on Siam Road is highly recommended by locals, and there is always a long queue every time you pass by. What is special is that they use the most ancient way - charcoal grilling to cook fried kway teow, so it is definitely full of wok flavor! Not only the wok flavor, but also the ingredients inside are very rich~ full of lard, eggs, blood clams (siham), and of course, the rice noodles with just the right thickness. Known as the "King of Penang Kway Teow", a plate of fried kway teow only costs RM6.50! Super generous~


Address: Jalan Siam, George Town, 10400 George Town, Pulau Pinang, Malaysia

Air Itam Bisu Laksa


A bowl of Asam Laksa with the perfect combination of sour, sweet and spicy, it’s right here at Air Itam Bisu ! This bowl of laksa is only RM4.50! Where else can you find noodles for less than RM5 nowadays! The sour and spicy taste is just right, and it’s full of fish and vegetables. After eating one bowl, you’ll want another!


Address: Air Itam Market Food Court, Penang

Authentic Qiaotou Fujian Noodles


The authentic Kiu Tou Hokkien Noodles stall is one of the best in Penang. Although it looks like a bowl of humble noodles, the soup base is very filling and only costs RM4 per bowl! You can also choose to add fish balls or pork offal as extra ingredients~

Address: Jalan CYChoy, 10300, Georgetown, Penang

Of course, the queue is always long, but the boss is quite quick, so you don't have to worry about waiting too long. Combining coconut milk, red beans, and the must-have Malacca syrup, this bowl of summer-cooling dessert is only RM2.70!

Natrah Nasi Kandar


This 60-year-old shop is a long-standing brand in Penang and the most popular Nasi Kandar. You can try their marinated meat with vegetables and curry sauce, as well as the must-try curry fish! If you want to visit this shop, remember to arrive early because they are only open for 3 hours and will be gone once they are sold out!


Address: Bee Ghah Cafe, 35, Jalan Teluk Kumbar, Kampung Teluk Kumbar, 11930 Teluk Kumbar, Penang

Famous Gani Pasembur


Pasembur is a kind of Indian Rojak. Most Chinese call it "fresh fish" or "blue fish". It is made with cucumber, potato, tofu, turnip, bean sprouts, shrimp cakes, seafood, and lots of sauce and peanut sauce. In Penang , Famous Gani Pasembur will prepare the "must-eat" fresh fish for you!

Address: 3, Medan Renong, Padang Kota Lama, Jalan Tun Syed Sheh Barakbah,, George Town, 10200 Georgetown, Pulau Pinang

Lebuh Chulia


This wonton noodle shop on Niu Gang Dong Street was visited by the famous gourmet Anthony Bourdain! In addition to being popular with locals, this wonton noodle shop is also quite suitable for foreigners, so you can usually see many foreigners eating wonton noodles here! The seemingly inconspicuous dish of noodles is filled with various essences and pork lard, the chewy noodles and the signature wontons, just looking at them will make you salivate~

Address: 300, Chulia St, Georgetown, 10200 George Town, Penang, Malaysia

Bayan Baru Market Food Court


Oyster omelette is not only available in Taiwan, Penang, Malaysia is also very delicious! Fresh oysters are paired with eggs and special sauces, a perfect balance! If you like spicy food, you can add a little garlic chili sauce to make it more appetizing!

Address: Lintang Mayang Pasir 4, Bandar Bayan Baru, 11900 Bayan Lepas, Pulau Pinang, Malaysia
